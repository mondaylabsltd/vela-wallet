# Introduction

Vela is a **self-custodial wallet** for ETH and ERC-20 tokens. You own your keys,
but there's no seed phrase to write down — you sign with a passkey, using your
face or fingerprint.

This documentation covers how to install Vela, create a wallet, move tokens, and
understand the security model behind it.

## The short version

- **Self-custodial.** Your funds are controlled by a key only you can use. Vela
  (the company) cannot move, freeze, or recover your money.
- **No seed phrase.** Your signing key lives in your device's secure hardware as
  a passkey. There is no twelve-word phrase to lose or have phished.
- **Open source.** The wallet is [public on GitHub](https://github.com/atshelchin/vela-wallet)
  so anyone can audit exactly what it does.

## Who it's for

Vela is built for people who want real self-custody without the footgun of seed
phrase management — and for people who've been burned by it before. If you can
unlock your phone, you can use Vela safely.
